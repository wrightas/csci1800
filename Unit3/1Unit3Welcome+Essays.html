Historical Background

Grace Hopper wanted computers to be accessible to regular people, without advanced training in mathematics. Hopper wrote the first compilers: A-0 (1951); MATH-MATIC (1955–57); FLOW-MATIC (1955–59).

"Accessibility" is an example of a moral value that directed Hopper's development of new technologies.
Grace Hopper had a vision for the future of computing. After programming the Harvard Mark I in World War II she was hired by the Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation to develop programs for the UNIVAC. Hopper had a PhD in Mathematics from ale University. But her vision was that programming a computer wouldn't require her level of expertise; she wanted computing to be accessible to regular people. "Accessibility to regular people" is an example of a moral value that Hopper wanted to direct the development of new technologies.

Essay Topic 1: Core Instuction

In an essay of between 675 and 825 words, pick one of the new technologies discussed in the Unit 3 readings and identify the most important moral value this was embedded in the technology.

Your thesis statement should [1] state your claim about the moral value and the technology and [2] explain why your reader should agree with the claim. Your essay should explain the meaning of the moral value.

Historical Background

Software Industry

Silicon Valley

Unit 3 describes two explosions of new small computer businesses: the development of software companies and the development of Silicon Valley manufacturing. In each case, the government played an important part. US "anti-trust" laws forced IBM to loosen its grip on software. And the US Military powerfully shaped software contractors like the RAND Corporation and Silicon Valley manufacturers like Fairchild Semiconductor.

Historical Background: Anti-Trust

  • Initially, software was included in the price of computers
  • Manufacturers like IBM dominated the market
  • Anti-Trust Laws
  • Main goals of Anti-Trust laws
    1. Increase competition, support free markets
    2. Support democracy, decrease corporate influence
Laws to limit powers of large corporations.

Historical Background: Anti-Trust

  • 1952: State of New York sues IBM
  • 1956: IBM agrees to “Consent Decree”
  • 1968: IBM unbundles software and services
    • Example: effects of unbundling for Life Insurance software
  • Pre-1968: Few independent packages
  • 1972: 81 vendors with 275 packages

Historical Background: Software

  • 1950s: Large contractors and first entrepreneurs
  • By mid-1960s: ~40-50 Large contractors and ~2,800 Smaller contractors
There was phenomenal growth in software companies in the 1960s. Your task is to assess how much government intervention was responsible

Historical Background: Silicon Valley

Historian Margaret O'Mara on Silicon Valley

Essay Topic 2: Core Instuction

Your thesis statement should [1] explicitly assess (judge) the claim and [2] explain why that is the correct assessment.
