3. Writing is a Process

Invention

Organization

Composition

Revision

  1. Invention: Active reading, take notes, discuss with classmates, idea generation, trial thesis
  2. Organization: Diagram argument, make concept map, make outlines
  3. Composition: paragraphs, write into outlines, go back to Invention, go back to Organization, iterate, compose drafts
  4. Revision: Peer-review, re-read, edit, correct, go back to earlier stages, polish

6. Grading

Your work will be graded by your TA. In order to produce uniform grading for all students, we do several things:

  • Rubric grading
  • Harmonization
  • Appeals
Rubric grading: all your work is evaluated according to a public "rubric" table that sets out the standard needed to achieve each grade level. When you receive a grade, you will see exactly how you were scored on the rubric.
Harmonization: the Teaching Team meets regularly to grade examples of student work together.
Appeals If you do not agree with a grade, your first step is to email your TA. If you two are unable to resolve the issue, any student can ask Dr. Wright to re-grade the assignment. (A re-grade reconsiders the assignment; compared to the initial grade, the new grade can be higher or lower.)

6. Grading Writing

Each Assignment has its own rubric. For evaluating writing, your writing will be evaluated on:

  • Argument
  • Evidence
  • Structure
  • Review
  • We also check for Academic Integrity, which gets its own video in Unit 0
Argument: Quality of argument, logic, and thesis statement
Evidence: Quality of evidence from the course material and use of evidence to support the argument
Structure Quality of structure and organization. Clarity and focus
Review: Quality of constructive comments on others' writing; ability to address criticism and improve from one draft to another
